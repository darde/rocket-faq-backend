## Loan Transfer & Early Access

### Transfer Details

**Q: Why is my loan being transferred?**
A: Loan transfers happen between servicers all the time for a variety of reasons, none of which are a reflection on you. We're devoted to providing the most valuable experience you've had as a homeowner.

**Q: Are there any changes to my loan I should know about?**
A: Typically, your loan number is the only thing that will change. Your loan terms, conditions, monthly payment, due date, and grace period will all remain the same. In some cases, small adjustments may occur due to factors such as escrow analysis, recent payments, and other research conducted during the onboarding of your loan. Any such changes will be reflected in your Rocket Mortgage monthly statements, and we'll be happy to explain them.

**Q: When will I receive my first billing statement?**
A: We will send you your first statement within 30 days of your transfer date. Please contact us if you have not received a statement by then. Want it sooner? Once your loan is fully onboarded, sign in to your online account to view your statement as soon as it's available.

**Q: Can I receive electronic paperless statements?**
A: Yes. Once your loan is onboarded and you have full online access, you can easily opt in for paperless from your account dashboard. Once you go Paperless, we'll notify you by email whenever you have a new document ready to view. Paperless is the fastest, easiest, and most secure way to receive important communications about your home loan.

**Q: What happens if I applied for or was approved for mortgage assistance?**
A: If you received mortgage assistance from your prior servicer, that will be reflected on your Rocket Mortgage loan. If you were in the process of applying for mortgage assistance, we will have all the information you've previously submitted, and you will not be required to restart the process.

**Q: What if I haven't received any emails from Rocket Mortgage yet?**
A: If you haven't received any emails from Rocket Mortgage with more details on your loan transfer, be sure to check your spam or junk folder. If you're still unable to find any emails, please contact us for assistance from a team member.

### Documents & Statements

**Q: How will the transfer impact my year-end tax statements?**
A: If your loan was transferred to Rocket Mortgage, you will still receive your year-end tax statement (Form 1098) in time to file your taxes. We will mail your 1098 no later than January 31 of the following year, and you can also access it electronically by logging into your Rocket account.

**Q: Where can I review the documents I receive from Rocket Mortgage?**
A: Once your loan transfer is complete, you'll be able to find all documents shared by Rocket Mortgage in the Documents section of your Rocket account. These documents will include billing statements, escrow analysis statements, tax documents, and more.

**Q: When does Rocket Mortgage send out billing statements?**
A: We'll send out your monthly statement no later than 2 weeks before your next due date.

### Taxes & Insurance During Transfer

**Q: Does the loan transfer have any impact on my escrow account?**
A: Your escrow funds with your previous servicer will transfer over automatically. From there, Rocket Mortgage will continue to pay your tax and/or insurance costs on your behalf. No action is needed from you.

**Q: Do I need to contact my insurance provider or tax authority?**
A: Yes. Please make sure Rocket Mortgage's Mortgagee Clause is listed correctly on your insurance policy. This allows your insurance provider to send us your annual policy renewal and keeps your account current.

Share the following Mortgagee Clause with your insurance provider:
```
Rocket Mortgage, LLC
Its Successors and/or Assigns
PO Box 7729
Springfield, OH 45501-7729
```

If you have an escrow account transferring over with your loan, you won't need to contact your tax authority — Rocket will do that for you.

**Q: I didn't have an escrow account before. Am I required to have one now?**
A: If you didn't have an escrow account with your previous servicer, your loan will remain non-escrow with Rocket Mortgage.

**Q: I have a third-party insurance policy. Will the transfer impact that policy?**
A: If you currently have optional insurance products such as life, accidental death, or disability insurance, it's important to note that Rocket Mortgage doesn't collect these premiums. Please contact your insurance provider for more details on how this transfer may affect your policy.

### Payment During Transfer

**Q: Will my current autopay schedule transfer over to my Rocket account?**
A: Most autopay schedules won't be eligible for transfer, so it's best to be prepared to re-enroll in autopay once your loan transfer is finalized. If your autopay is eligible, you'll see confirmation of its successful transfer in your Rocket account. If your autopay was successfully transferred, you may see a delay in the first payment while the transfer is finalized – but rest assured that payment will be drafted and any potential delay won't result in any late fees.

**Q: What if I already scheduled my next payment with my previous servicer?**
A: If you already sent a payment to your previous servicer, they'll forward it to Rocket Mortgage without any penalty to you. If your payment is sent back to you, you can simply send the payment directly to Rocket Mortgage. If there's any delay, don't worry – you won't be charged late fees or see any impact on your credit report for the next 60 days.

**Q: How long is the grace period for making payments during this transfer?**
A: To ensure a smooth transition in your mortgage payments, there is a 60-day grace period so Rocket can receive any scheduled one-time payments or autopay with your previous servicer. During this time, we won't charge late fees and there will be no impact to your credit.

**Q: What options are available for making payments with Rocket Mortgage?**
A: Paying online with your Rocket account is safe and quick. You'll have the option to set up autopay or make one-time payments. If online payments aren't right for you, you can also mail in a check or use bank bill pay.

**Q: How do I mail a payment to Rocket Mortgage?**
A: You can mail your monthly payment using a check or money order payable to "Rocket Mortgage." Please be sure to include your new Rocket Mortgage loan number on it. (Note: Mailing address will be provided based on your loan location)

**Q: What if I've been using bank bill pay with my previous servicer?**
A: Please contact your bank as soon as possible to set up payments to Rocket Mortgage. This will help avoid any issues in the future.

**Q: I've made my first payment to Rocket Mortgage. When will it post to my account?**
A: All payments will be posted to your account within 10 days of your transfer date. We will credit you with making your payment on the day we receive it. These delays will not result in any late fees or negative credit impacts.

**Q: I'm ready to pay off my mortgage. How do I get a payoff quote from Rocket Mortgage?**
A: Once your onboarding is finalized, you can request a payoff quote online or by phone. To get started, give us a call at (800) 508-0944. After that, you can respond to the interactive prompts with your voice or by pressing the keys on your touchtone phone.

- **One-time payments**: Schedule a one-time payment for:

  - Partial payment or custom amount

### Autopay

**Q: What are the benefits of autopay?**
A: Autopay is a repeating automatic payment that comes out of your bank account each month on the date you choose. Autopay is free to use and has many advantages:

- **Set it and forget it**: After you set up autopay, you'll have peace of mind knowing you'll never miss a payment.
- **Avoid late fees**: Automatic payments are always made on time.
- **Adjust automatically**: If your payment changes due to escrow changes, autopay will automatically adjust.
- **Choose your date**: Select any payment date between the 1st and the 16th of the month.

**Q: How can I set up autopay?**
A: You can set up our free autopay service from the payment center. You can choose a date between the 1st and 16th of the month, but you won't be able to schedule same-day autopay. For example, you can set up autopay on January 6 to start on January 7. But you can't set up autopay on January 6 to start on January 6.

To be eligible, you must be caught up on all previous months' payments and due for the current month. For example, to set up autopay for February, you'll need to have paid January's payment and be due for February.

**Q: How do I edit or cancel autopay?**
A: You can manage autopay settings from the payment center. To edit or cancel any autopay transaction, you'll need at least one business day before the scheduled payment date. For example, if your autopay is scheduled for today, you cannot change it today; it would be too late to take effect.

**Q: If my monthly payment amount changes because of taxes, insurance, or rate adjustment, will you update my autopay?**
A: That depends on how you've set up automatic payments.

- **Yes for Rocket Mortgage autopay**: If you've set up autopay through Rocket Mortgage and your monthly payment changes because of taxes, insurance, or a rate adjustment, then we'll automatically update your autopay amount for you.

- **No for bank bill pay**: If you've set up automatic bill pay through your bank, then you'll need to contact your bank to adjust the payment amount.

### Biweekly Payments

**Q: How can I set up biweekly autopay?**
A: To set up our free biweekly autopay service, go to the payment center and follow these steps:

1. Make sure you're paid ahead, meaning you've already paid next month's payment.
2. Choose a date between the 1st and 14th of next month.
3. A half payment will draft every 14 days from the date you select.

**Q: What are the benefits of paying my mortgage biweekly instead of monthly?**
A: Biweekly (every-other-week) payments help you pay off your mortgage faster and pay less interest over time. Also, they can make it easier to budget if you're paid on the same schedule.

**Q: How do biweekly payments work?**
A: Biweekly payments split your monthly payment into two, so they come out of your bank account every two weeks. Each payment equals half of your normal monthly payment.

After you make your first payment, we hold onto it until we get your second one. Then, we apply both payments to your loan at once to pay your full monthly amount due.

Because of the calendar, there are two months a year when you'd make three half payments. The third payment is applied directly to your loan principal. That's like the equivalent of making one extra payment directly to your mortgage balance each year.

### One-Time Payments

**Q: How can I make a one-time payment?**
A: You have the flexibility to schedule a one-time payment through Rocket Mortgage for any of these scenarios:

- **Principal only**: This goes directly toward your principal balance.
- **Partial payment or custom amount**: The funds won't be applied until you make additional payments that equal your full monthly amount.
- **Extra escrow**: This goes directly toward your escrow account.

**Q: How do I edit or cancel a one-time payment?**
A: To edit the payment amount or bank account, go to the Payment Center. To change the payment date, you'll have to cancel the payment and schedule a new one. Edits or cancellations can be made until 11:30 p.m. ET on the scheduled payment date.

**Q: How can I make an additional principal payment?**
A: You can make additional principal payments online through the payment center by selecting "Principal Only" as your payment option.

If you prefer to mail a check for additional principal payments, send it to:
```

Attn: Servicing Team
635 Woodward Ave
Detroit, MI 48226
```
Please include your loan number on the check and indicate that the payment is for principal only.

### Payment Processing & Timing

**Q: If I schedule a payment with Rocket Mortgage, when will it draft from my bank account?**
A: Depending on your scheduled payment date and your banking institution, it can take one to three business days (not including holidays) for payments to show in your bank account.

**Q: How long is my grace period?**
A: A grace period is the time after your due date when you can still make a payment without being charged a late fee. Grace periods are different depending on your loan type. Find your grace period in your closing package.

**Q: When are payments officially reported as late to the credit bureaus?**
A: We report payments as late to credit bureaus once the month your payment is due has completely passed. So, if you don't make your January payment by January 31, that payment will be reported as late on the next business day.

**Q: When are late fees charged?**
A: If your monthly payment hasn't posted by the end of your grace period, then we'll charge a late fee after your grace period ends.

**Q: How much are late fees?**
A: Generally, most late payment fees range from 4%-6% of your total monthly payment. You can find your late fee amount on your latest billing statement.

**Q: What's a non-sufficient funds (NSF) fee?**
A: If your bank account doesn't have enough money to cover your scheduled mortgage payment, then your bank will reject the payment. We'll try to process the mortgage payment again, so you won't need to schedule another payment at that time. If the second attempt fails, then we'll charge you a non-sufficient funds (NSF) fee and you'll need to schedule a new payment.

## Year-End Tax Statements (1098)

### Receiving Your 1098

**Q: How will I get my 1098?**
A: All Rocket Mortgage clients will receive their 1098 online on January 2. If you are not enrolled in paperless, you will also receive your 1098 by mail. Go paperless by December 31 to get your 1098 online only.

**Q: When can I expect to receive my 1098 tax statement?**
A: Your 1098 tax statement will be available in your Rocket Account after the first business day in January. Your 1098 will also be placed in the mail no later than January 31 if you're enrolled to receive a physical copy.

**Q: How can I receive a paper copy of my 1098 tax statement?**
A: You can print a copy of your 1098 tax statement from your Documents page once it's ready. And if you're not enrolled in paperless 1098s, you can expect a copy in the mail.

**Q: How do I enroll in paperless 1098 tax statements?**
A: Enroll in paperless statements under your Communication Preferences by December 30.

**Q: When's the last date I can change my paperless preferences for 1098 tax statements?**
A: The last day to make changes to your paperless preferences is December 30.

**Q: I didn't get a 1098. What does that mean?**
A: You'll only receive a 1098 if you made at least one full payment and paid more than $600 in interest during the tax year. If your loan was in forbearance, you'll only get a 1098 if you made qualifying payments. Not sure if you should have received a 1098? Reach out and we'll review your situation.

### Multiple Loans & Transfers

**Q: I refinanced this year. Will I have multiple 1098s?**
A: Yes. Each time you refinance, you get a new loan number with a separate 1098 statement.

- **If you refinanced a Rocket Mortgage loan**: You'll have 1098 statements for both your old and new loan numbers. Be sure to review and save both statements.
- **If you refinanced with another lender**: You'll need to contact this lender for information about the 1098 for that loan.

**Q: I had more than one loan or my loan was transferred to a different servicer. How many 1098s will I receive?**
A: You'll receive a 1098 for each loan that you had open and paid interest toward in the previous year. If your other loans were with Rocket Mortgage, those 1098s are available in the Documents section of your Rocket account.

If your other loans were with a different mortgage company, they would provide you with a 1098. If your loan was transferred to another servicer, you'll receive a 1098 from each servicer.

**Q: My loan was acquired by Rocket Mortgage, or transferred to a new servicer in the last year. Will I have multiple 1098s?**
A: Yes, you'll receive one statement from your old servicer and one from your new servicer. So, if your loan transferred to or from Rocket Mortgage in the last year, you can expect 1098 statements from both Rocket Mortgage and the other servicer.

If you have questions about the 1098 from your old servicer, you'll need to contact them for more information.

**Q: I've refinanced my loan during this year, will I be receiving multiple 1098 tax statements?**
A: Yes, each time you refinance you're reassigned a new loan number and you'll receive a separate 1098 tax statement for each loan number. If you have questions about filing your taxes, we recommend reaching out to a tax professional.

### Understanding Your 1098

**Q: Why doesn't the outstanding mortgage principal listed in box 2 line up with my current unpaid principal balance?**
A: The balance listed in box 2 isn't your current principal balance. It's the balance of your loan on January 1 of the previous year. If your loan was originated in the previous year, it's the original balance of your loan.

This can differ depending on certain factors:
- If your loan originated this year, box 2 lists the original balance of the loan.
- If your loan was transferred to Rocket Mortgage this year, box 2 lists the balance of your loan at the date of transfer.

**Q: I paid more toward my interest than what is showing. Am I able to claim the additional interest?**
A: Per IRS regulation, you can only claim mortgage interest for 13 months of payments, up to January of the current year. Mortgage interest is paid in arrears, so the interest paid on the January payment was accrued in December of the previous year.

**Q: How can I claim the interest on my income taxes if my name is not listed on the 1098 tax statement?**
A: We recommend speaking with a tax advisor about the best thing to do in your situation – but typically you can take the deduction as long as another person, on the mortgage or otherwise, isn't making a claim to the same deduction.

Rocket Mortgage will put the person listed first on the mortgage on the 1098 and this cannot be changed. Depending on your situation, you may be able to claim the interest provided on the statement. If you have questions about what you can claim, we recommend speaking with a tax professional.

**Q: I recently changed my name, but my 1098 has my old name on it. How can I fix this?**
A: If the name you're filing your taxes under does not currently match the name on your mortgage, you can expect a notice in the mail. This is simply a request to send us documentation so we can update our records for you.

### Special Situations

**Q: I have a VA loan and I don't pay mortgage insurance. Why does box 5 have a number listed?**
A: Some VA loans have a funding fee that's included at origination, which the IRS considers to be a mortgage insurance payment. Box 5 includes the charges for either your full VA funding fee amount if you paid up front or the amount that you paid in the past year if you build it into your balance. You can review more details in your Closing Disclosure.

**Q: I don't have a VA loan and I don't pay mortgage insurance. Why does box 5 have a number listed?**
A: You have Lender Paid Mortgage Insurance (LPMI) on your loan. This was built into the origination of your loan, which you can review in your Closing Disclosure.

**Q: I paid points at origination, but they are not listed. Where would that information be?**
A: Only points paid on a primary residence purchase loan are listed on the 1098. Any points paid on a different loan type are listed on your origination closing disclosure at the top of page 2, under section A "origination charges," which can be used in your income tax preparation.

1098 statements only list points paid on primary residence purchase loans. However, any points paid on a different loan type can be used in your income tax preparation. Rocket Mortgage can't disclose points paid at origination if the loan was closed with a different lender. These points are listed on page 2 of your Closing Disclosure under section A, Origination Charges. You can find the disclosure within the Closing Package in your Documents.

**Q: Page 2 of my 1098 is incorrect. How do I get it fixed?**
A: A new 1098 doesn't need to be reissued for a page 2 correction because it's listed for information purposes and is not reported to the IRS.

**Q: I paid off my loan during this past year, how can I make sure that I receive my 1098 tax statement to my new address?**
A: Verify your mailing address in your settings, or avoid waiting for the mail and sign up for paperless 1098 tax statements in your Communication Preferences.

**Q: Why does my 1098 tax statement look different than last year?**
A: The amount you pay may vary from year to year, usually due to a change in your interest rate or in the number of payments we received from you during the calendar year. If you were in a forbearance or missed a payment and have not caught the missed payment(s) up with the calendar year, your interest could be lower.

### Other Tax Forms

**Q: Why didn't I receive a Form 1099-C this year when I received one last year?**
A: The IRS only requires us to send a 1099-C (Cancellation of Debt) when we cancel $600 or more of your principal balance in a calendar year.

**Q: Why did I receive a 1099-MISC?**
A: If you received any promotional offers from closing, such as AMEX rewards, we are required to send a 1099-MISC for the cost.

**Q: I sold my home. How do I get my 1099-S?**
A: If you sold your home, you'll need to contact the title company that assisted with the sale in order to obtain a copy of your 1099-S.

**Q: Is there additional documentation I may need to go along with my 1098 tax statement for my taxes?**
A: A copy of your Closing Disclosure will likely be needed when completing your taxes, which you can find in your closing package in your documents page. For any additional documents you may need, we recommend checking with your tax professional.

## Escrow Account

### Understanding Escrow

**Q: What is an escrow account?**
A: With an escrow account, you don't have to worry about managing your taxes and insurance. We do it for you. Instead of paying your taxes and insurance in large lump sums, escrow breaks them into more manageable payments. These smaller payments are rolled into your monthly mortgage payment.

**Q: What items are included in my Escrow Account?**
A: Items that are typically included in an Escrow Account:
- Property taxes
- Homeowners Insurance
- Flood insurance (if applicable)
- Wind insurance (if applicable)
- Mortgage insurance (if applicable)

**Q: How do I know if I have an escrow account setup for my loan?**
A: If you have an escrow account set up for your loan, your Mortgage Statement and closing Disclosure will include a breakdown for the Escrow Account. The escrow information section of your account will also feature your escrow analysis date and additional escrow information once the analysis is completed.

### Escrow Analysis

**Q: What is an escrow analysis?**
A: Your monthly escrow payment is determined by a yearly escrow analysis. This accounts for any changes to your taxes and/or insurance premiums. If we're collecting too little based on those changes, we'll adjust your payment to make up the difference. If there's too much money in your account due to those changes, we'll send you a refund.

An escrow analysis helps us calculate what your monthly escrow payment should be to match your yearly taxes/insurance bills. We do this analysis every year to make sure we're collecting what's expected to be paid in the next escrow cycle. If we find we're collecting too much or too little, your monthly payments may change as needed.

**Q: How often is an escrow analysis completed for my loan?**
A: An escrow analysis is completed once a year. You can see when your escrow analysis is completed by viewing your escrow information page.

An escrow analysis typically happens once a year following the state schedule. Your next analysis is scheduled for the scheduled date. You can visit your Escrow page to check the date of your next escrow analysis.

**Q: An escrow analysis was just completed for my account and my payment went up. Why?**
A: Your payment would have increased due to your taxes and/or insurance premium increasing, meaning we need to collect more funds for the next disbursement. To find out more about why your taxes and/or insurance premium increased, we recommend contacting your tax authority or insurance provider directly.

**Q: What's the math behind my anticipated expenses for the year?**
A: We take your tax and insurance bills from last year — along with your minimum required balance — and use them to calculate your expenses for the year ahead.

**Q: What is my minimum required balance?**
A: Your minimum required escrow balance is set by the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA), your mortgage contract or state law. That amount is the lowest point your escrow balance can reach at any time. An escrow shortage happens when your escrow balance falls below the minimum required balance. This is often because of changes in property taxes or insurance expenses.

**Q: How is my minimum required balance calculated?**
A: Your minimum required balance is calculated using your annual property taxes and homeowners insurance. The combined cost of your property taxes and homeowners insurance is first broken down into a monthly total, and then multiplied by 12.

### Escrow Shortage & Surplus

**Q: My Escrow Account showed I have a shortage. How can I pay this?**
A: You can make this payment directly through your payment center:
- You would need to select Escrow Only under Payment Options.
- Once this is clicked, the Payment Amount will reflect Escrow Only Amount.
- The Payment Total will also reflect Escrow Only.

Once the payment has been submitted, it will then appear under the Upcoming Scheduled Payments in your payment center.

**Q: Why did my expenses go down?**
A: To get a better understanding of why your property taxes and homeowners insurance premiums have gone down, speak with your tax authority and insurance provider. You can find their contact info on your Escrow page.

### Escrow Account Closure

**Q: What happens to the money in my escrow account when my loan has been paid in full?**
A: You'll be refunded any money that's remaining in your account. If you are refinancing with us, the escrow balance may be used to bring down the new loan's principal balance.

**Q: Once my loan balance has been fully repaid, what happens to any money left in my escrow account?**
A: Any money remaining in your account when you pay off your total loan balance is refunded to you in the form of a check. If you're refinancing with us, the remaining escrow balance may also be used to bring down the new loan's principal balance.

## Homeowners Insurance

### Understanding Homeowners Insurance

**Q: What is homeowner's insurance premium?**
A: Homeowners insurance commonly referred to as hazard insurance, which is the basic insurance every homeowner must have on their home. It is also the premium amount you must pay the insurance company each year for the coverage on your home. In certain situations, other types of insurance like wind or flood insurance are also required.

**Q: What is homeowners insurance (HOI)?**
A: Homeowners Insurance (sometimes called hazard insurance) is coverage for things like repairs, or rebuilding your home, after damage. This usually includes events like fires, smoke, theft, or a falling tree. Every homeowner must have homeowners insurance on their home.

### Premium Changes & Payment

**Q: Is it possible for my homeowner's insurance premium to change?**
A: Yes, your homeowner's insurance premium can change over time. This can happen for several reasons, such as changes in your coverage, increases in the cost to repair or rebuild your home, inflation, or if you file a claim. Sometimes, even changes in your area – like increased risk of natural disasters – can affect your premium.

If your insurance is paid through an escrow account with your mortgage, your monthly payment may also adjust to reflect any changes in your premium.

Your homeowners premium amount can change each year when your policy renews. Renewal happens automatically unless you switch to a new provider. For questions regarding your premium potentially changing, we recommend contacting your licensed insurance agent.

**Q: My homeowners insurance premiums just increased. Why did this happen?**

## Mortgage Insurance (PMI/MIP)

### General Information

**Q: What is mortgage insurance?**
A: Mortgage insurance is insurance coverage that helps protect a lender's losses if you stop making payments on your loan. Client-paid mortgage insurance is a portion of your monthly payment and is included in escrow. It is often confused with homeowner's insurance or thought to be an insurance that assists clients who experience a financial hardship. Mortgage insurance does not pay off a loan in the event of loss of employment or the death of a borrower. We recommend that you contact your current homeowner's insurance company for that type of policy.

Mortgage insurance varies depending on what loan type you have. FHA loans have mortgage insurance premium (MIP). Conventional loans may have private mortgage insurance (PMI). Understanding your loan type will help guide your path through mortgage insurance. Refer to your closing documents – your loan type will be checked.

**Q: What is mortgage insurance (MI)?**
A: Mortgage insurance is insurance coverage that helps protect a lender's losses if you stop making payments on your loan. Client-paid mortgage insurance is a portion of your monthly payment and is included in escrow. It is often confused with homeowner's insurance or thought to be an insurance that assists clients who experience a financial hardship. Mortgage insurance does not payoff a loan in the event of loss of employment or the death of a borrower. We recommend that you contact your current homeowner's insurance company for that type of policy.

Mortgage insurance varies depending on what loan type you have. FHA loans have mortgage insurance premium (MIP). Conventional loans may have private mortgage insurance (PMI). Understanding your loan type will help guide your path through mortgage insurance.

**Q: Why do I have mortgage insurance?**
A: Conventional mortgages often require PMI when less than 20% of a home's purchase price is provided as a down payment or a home has less than 20% equity (determined by loan to value, which is the amount owed on the home divided by home value) during a refinance. If your loan was originated to purchase your home, the value is based on the lesser of the appraised amount or the purchase price.

All newly originated FHA loans have MIP for at least a portion of the loan duration, if not all of it. Length of MIP on an FHA loan is determined by equity at origination. FHA loans on homes with less than 10% equity at origination require MIP for the life of the loan. FHA loans on homes that have more than 10% equity will pay MIP for the first 11 years of the loan.

Information detailing Mortgage Insurance, if applicable to your loan, was disclosed at origination and made available in your closing package via the Notice Concerning Private Mortgage Insurance (conventional loans) or Important Notice to Homebuyer documents (FHA loans).

**Q: Can I pay more toward my loan in order to meet the equity requirement sooner?**
A: You can pay extra toward your balance to meet the required 22% equity (or 78% loan to value) sooner than your original amortization schedule, but FHA Mortgage Insurance cannot be removed sooner than the minimum year requirement (which is dependent on when your FHA case number was assigned).

You can pay extra to meet the required 22% equity (or 78% loan to value) sooner than your original amortization schedule. But, FHA mortgage insurance can't be removed sooner than the minimum year requirement (which is dependent on when your FHA case number was assigned).

### FHA Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP)

**Q: Can I remove mortgage insurance (FHA)?**
A: There are several ways that Mortgage Insurance can be removed. FHA loans are dependent on the day your FHA Case Number (created during the origination process) was assigned. The date of your FHA case number assignment and the closing of your loan may not be the same date. A most recent twelve month on time payment history will also be required.

If your FHA case number was assigned on or before June 2, 2013 and the original term of your loan was greater than 15 years, MIP would be removed after both of the following criteria are met:
- More than 22% equity in the home (based on the original appraised value for a refinance, or the lesser of the purchase price or appraised value for a purchase)
- Mortgage insurance paid for five years

If your FHA case number was assigned on or before June 2, 2013 and the original term of your loan was less than 15 years, MIP would be removed with the following criteria:
- More than 22% equity in the home based on the original amortization schedule

If your FHA case number was assigned on or after June 3, 2013, and you had more than 10% equity at origination, the following requirements need to be met for MIP removal:
- Mortgage insurance paid for 11 years

If your FHA case number was assigned on or after June 3, 2013, and you had less than 10% equity at origination, you must pay mortgage insurance for the life of your loan. You do have the option of refinancing into a loan that could help you remove mortgage insurance.

**Q: How is my MIP payment amount determined?**
A: MIP on FHA loans is variable depending on the factors of your loan. Annual MIP payments run approximately 0.45% – 1.05% of your base loan amount. The percentage is determined by the length of your mortgage, your loan-to-value (LTV) ratio (the amount of your loan size divided by the value of your home at origination, expressed as a percentage), your total mortgage amount and the size of your down payment.

**Q: What is upfront mortgage insurance?**
A: Upfront Mortgage Insurance Premium (UFMIP) is a one-time payment charge at origination on FHA loans which normally amounts to 1.75% of your base loan amount. Refer to the closing disclosure in your My Documents page to determine if you paid UFMIP at closing or if it was including in your new loan amount.

**Q: Can I remove upfront mortgage insurance?**
A: No. UFMIP is a one-time transaction paid at origination.

### Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) - Conventional Loans

**Q: Why do I need private mortgage insurance (PMI)?**
A: You're paying PMI on your loan because you either put less than 20% down on your home purchase, or had less than 20% equity when you refinanced.

**Q: Can I remove mortgage insurance (PMI)?**
A: Mortgage insurance can be removed from your conventional loan in various ways. The methods which can be used for removal are dependent on attributes of the origination of your loan. The following information applies if your home is a single-family residence and used as your primary residence or second home. A clean payment history is also required for removal. Delinquent mortgage payments made in the prior 12 to 24 months can prevent mortgage insurance removal. You may be able to remove your private mortgage insurance in the following ways:

**Your auto termination date:**
- The loan to value on your property reaches 78%, which means you've earned 22% equity in your home based on the original amortization schedule (and you didn't make extra payments to get it there). This date is determined at the time of closing and can be found on your "notice to home buyer concerning mortgage insurance" document.
- When your mortgage is paid through the mid-point of your amortized loan (example: 180 months into 360 term), but your loan to value is not yet 78%, your mortgage insurance may be removed the following month.

**Paying more toward your principal in Order to Meet the Equity Requirement Sooner:**
- Yes, you can pay more toward your principal balance to meet the equity requirement sooner and request that PMI is removed sooner than planned. If you have paid your principal down to 80% loan to original value, you can request a home valuation to verify that your home didn't lose value since closing. There may be a cost to the valuation that you may incur.

**An Increase in your home's value:**
- **If your loan is less than 2 years old:** you'll need to make home improvements to increase your property value. You may need to provide us with details of significant home improvements completed since closing on your loan. A natural increase in value within the first two years of the loan, will not qualify for PMI removal.
  - Substantial improvements include, but are not limited to: Adding a bathroom, Adding a deck, Adding square footage, Kitchen renovations

- **If your loan is older than two years:** and you feel your home has gone up in value, you can request a home evaluation, however the value must come back at 75% loan to value. (Not applicable in Minnesota. Not applicable to loans older than 5 years or Freddie Mac loans over 2 years with improvements).

PMI removal cannot occur early without a new value being acquired. Appraisals ordered by an external party outside of Rocket Mortgage cannot be used for the new value.

**Q: Does it matter how long I've been making payments on my loan?**
A: Yes. If you've been paying less than two years, major home improvements are required to be eligible for early removal. If you've been paying over two years, you may be able to use your property's new value to become eligible. A property valuation is required to confirm eligibility in both cases.

**Q: What home improvements can help me remove PMI early?**
A: Substantial improvements include, but are not limited to:
- Significant structural alterations, like adding square footage
- Kitchen renovations
- Adding solar panels
- Construction that required permits
- Adding new features that previously did not exist, like a bathtub, hardwood flooring, or central AC
- Replacing components or appliances with energy efficient upgrades

All home improvements are subject to investor approval and must be completed post-close.

**Q: How is my PMI amount determined?**
A: PMI, like other types of insurance, is based on insurance rates that can change daily. PMI typically costs 0.5% – 1% of your loan amount per year. Your Mortgage Insurance may decline over the life of the loan on an annual basis. Your loan to value and credit score at origination may also impact your PMI rate.

**Q: What's the cost of a home valuation?**
A: The cost of a home valuation can vary based on your location and loan type. Our experts are here to make it easy – call us for an evaluation and we'll walk you through your exact cost.

## Insurance Claims

### Claim Checks

**Q: Why is my insurance claim check also made payable to Rocket Mortgage?**
A: When you have a mortgage, your lender has a financial interest in your property. This interest is only secured when the property's value meets or exceeds the loan amount owed on it. Since insurance checks for property damage are meant to pay for repairs to bring your property back to its original value, lenders want to make sure repairs are completed satisfactorily.

**Q: What should I do if I get a claim check made payable to QuickenLoans, not Rocket Mortgage?**
A: QuickenLoans is our former company name, and we still accept and endorse checks made payable to QuickenLoans. We'll process it the same way as those made payable to Rocket Mortgage.

**Q: When can I expect to receive my insurance claim funds from Rocket Mortgage?**
A: How long it takes to receive funds can vary from a few days to a few weeks depending on your specific situation. We'll release funds to you after we receive the insurance claim check, you've provided any documents we need, and any required property inspections are complete.

**Q: Why does Rocket Mortgage release funds for larger claim checks in separate payments?**
A: For larger insurance claims, funds may be released in separate payments to ensure that work is completed in line with a set schedule. This protects you from contractors who may otherwise demand payment upfront and then walk away from the project. It also protects the lender's interest in your property.

**Q: Can I use the money from my insurance claim check to make a payment on my mortgage?**
A: The insurance company expects the funds from the claim check to be used to make repairs to your property. If you've already completed the needed repairs, have your property inspected through us, and after funds are released to you for that purpose, apply them toward your mortgage payment.

**Q: What can I do to avoid contractor fraud?**

## Forbearance & Payment Assistance

**Q: What are my options for catching up on payments that were paused?**

- With a **repayment plan**, we'll add part of your past-due amount to your regular mortgage payment each month.
- With a **deferral or partial claim**, we'll set all or part of your past-due amount aside to be paid later. It will be due when you pay off your mortgage, sell your home or refinance. You won't be charged any interest on the deferred balance.

- We'll automatically extend your forbearance another 3 months if you can't resume payments.

After you've used 6 months of forbearance, you can extend your forbearance again if you can't resume payments. Just check in from your RocketMortgage.com dashboard to let us know. If you still need assistance after 12 months of forbearance, we'll help you figure out your options.

## HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit)

### Draw Period

**Q: What is a draw period?**
A: A draw period is the time frame when you're allowed to access, or draw from, the credit available on your home equity line. Most home equity lines of credit (HELOCs) have a 120-month (10-year) draw period beginning on the date of your credit agreement.

**Q: Is there a limit to the number of draws I can make on my line of credit?**
A: There's no limit on the number of draws you can make from your account – but you can't draw more money than the available amount on your credit line.

**Q: When I pay to principal during my draw period, when will those funds be available?**
A: Most payments made to principal are held for ten calendar days. Once successfully cleared, those funds are available. Certified funds clear in one day while other payments will take ten.
